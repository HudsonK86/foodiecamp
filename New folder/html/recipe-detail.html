Traditional Nasi Lemak

4.8 (126 reviews)
Posted by Chef Maria Dec 10, 2024

Cuisine Type

Malay

Difficulty

Medium

Cooking Time

45 mins

Servings

4 people

Description

Nasi Lemak is Malaysia's national dish, featuring fragrant coconut rice served with sambal, anchovies, peanuts, boiled egg, and cucumber. This authentic recipe brings together the perfect balance of flavors that make this dish so beloved across Southeast Asia. The secret lies in cooking the rice with coconut milk and pandan leaves, which gives it its distinctive aroma and creamy texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 1 can coconut milk (400ml)
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 boiled eggs
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/2 cup sambal paste
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1/2 cup fried anchovies

Cooking Instructions

1

Rinse the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.

2

In a rice cooker or heavy-bottomed pot, combine the rice, coconut milk, pandan leaves, salt, and water. Mix gently.

3

Cook the rice according to your rice cooker instructions, or if using a pot, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes.

4

While the rice is cooking, prepare the sambal by heating it in a pan until fragrant. Set aside.

5

Once the rice is done, remove the pandan leaves and fluff with a fork.

6

Serve the coconut rice with sambal, boiled eggs, sliced cucumber, roasted peanuts, and fried anchovies arranged beautifully on the plate.
